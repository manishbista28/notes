Vincristine

Use before drugs that affect liver
Do not use with CYP3A inhibitors like azole antifungals
Affects gut and nervous system; check motor over sensory function as it is less subjective

So far I haven't found anything peculiar or outstanding with vincristine.

Cytarabine

- low/standard dose given for ALL, high for AML
- eviq's hyper CVAD Induction 2 however includes high dose cytarabine
- in standard dose, around 80% of the drug is deamined into Ara-U from Ara-C
- the rest makes its way inside cells
- mostly marrow supression and mucosal toxicity
- given subcutaneously to ensure drug's effect stays for long

- also given intrathecally
- CSF lacks cytidine deaminase enzyme, so elimination half life is longer compared to minutes in plasma
- protocols almost always mandate the co-administration of an intrathecal or systemic corticosteroid (typically dexamethasone or hydrocortisone) alongside the cytarabine.
- Flu like constellation of fever 6 to 12 hours after drug is administered
- peak myleosupression after 7 to 14 days


Anthracyclines (Daunorubicin / Doxorubicin)
- it is cell cycle phase nonspecific
- leukemia blasts that are trying to divide or repair DNA are pushed towards apoptosis
- they have cumulative, dose limiting cardiac toxicity
- cardiotoxicity
- hepatic metabolism, biliary excretion
- Drop in relatiive Global Longitudinal Strain (GLS) > 15% from baseline

Corticosteroid (Dexamethasone)
- drug binds with receptor, pushes lymphoid cells into cell-cycle arrest and apoptosis
- steroids can trigger in prephase stage TLS even before full chemo
- infection masking, hyperglycemia, osteonecrosis, adrenal supression
- taper off instead of cut off

Cyclophosphomides
- non phase specific cytotoxic agent
- Acrolein is urotoxic, Phosphoramide Mustard is the alkylator
- Has both Activating and Detoxifying pathways
- Hematopoietic stem cells, intestinal epithelium, and hepatic tissues express high levels of ALDH1A1, affording them relative protection. Conversely, malignant lymphoblasts possess minimal ALDH activity, leaving them highly vulnerable to the accumulation of phosphoramide mustard.
- Use Mesna, GCSF
- Quote:
Syndrome of Inappropriate Antidiuretic Hormone (SIADH)
Causative Variable: High doses of cyclophosphamide or its metabolites exert a direct effect on the renal distal tubules, impairing free water clearance.

Clinical Management Conflict: This creates a tight clinical paradox: the patient requires massive fluid volumes to prevent acrolein-induced hemorrhagic cystitis, but SIADH impairs water excretion, risking profound, acute hyponatremia and subsequent seizures. Continuous serum and urine osmolality, alongside meticulous electrolyte monitoring, are required.
