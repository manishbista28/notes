Continuing on Methotrexate Toxicity Levels from log_1.

1. Around 65-80 micromoles of plasma Mtx concentration at 24h mark is sufficient.
2. Mtx level peaked at 2h and stabilized at 6h. Protocols exist to adapt Mtx level within this 24h window such that peak toxicity doesn't exceed 100 micromoles. This is useful for high risk patients.
3. 36, 42 and 48h marks are the same as in COG protocol.
4. Lecucovorin rescue depends on the amount of Mtx in circulation. They compete for the same cellular uptake. So you need for Leucovorin if Mtx concentration is also high.
5. High leucovorin dose can definitely counteract the effect of Mtx and should be given with caution. Increases relapse risk by 22 percent if leucovorin is over-dosed.
6. Standard protocols suggest not delaying rescue more than 42 and sometimes 36h as it increases risk of toxicity significantly. 
7. The amount of leucovorin to rescue with depends on Mtx levels, this can range from 15 to 30 mg given every 6 hours.
8. Protocols where Mtx was given in a shorter duration i.e. 5 g/sq_m over less than 24h made use of leucovorin rescue at 24h mark. Otherwise, all standard approach suggest 36h-42h mark.
9. Carefully personalized Mtx dosing has the benefit of avoiding critical organ injury and not requiring early rescue. Good balance.
